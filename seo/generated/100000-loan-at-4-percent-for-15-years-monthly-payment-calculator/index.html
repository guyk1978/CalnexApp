Is a 15-year loan worth it at 4%?

Worth it when $739.69 fits net cash flow after reserves—not when it maxes out DTI. You trade $262.27/mo versus a 30-year schedule for about $38,725.68 less lifetime interest.

Scenario lens: 5.5% rate and $100.00 extra principal

If your quote drifts to 5.5% on the same 15-year structure, payment rises to $817.08 (+$77.40/mo) and lifetime interest approaches $47,075.02—about $13,931.20 more than this 4% baseline.

Adding $100.00/month to principal (total outflow ~$839.69) targets payoff around month 152 and cuts scheduled interest toward $27,624.76.

Refinancing and affordability

Refi only if break-even months fit your hold period (refinance break-even). Affordability includes escrows and other debts—see DTI qualification.

Who this fits

  • Stable income households prioritizing total interest over minimum payment.
  • Borrowers with a verified plan to stay past break-even after a refi.

Pause if emergency savings are thin or high-APR unsecured debt should clear first.

Risks of a shorter amortization

Main risk is liquidity: extra principal is hard to access without borrowing again. Confirm prepayment rules on your note.

Refinance scenario: You owe $100,000.00 at a higher legacy rate. A 4% / 15-year offer at $739.69 only wins if closing costs divide into true monthly savings before you sell—see refinance break-even.

Frequently asked questions

What is the monthly payment on $100,000.00 at 4% for 15 years?

About $739.69 per month in principal and interest for 180 on-time payments (total repayment about $133,143.83).

How much total interest will I pay?

Roughly $33,143.83 over the full term if you do not prepay.

How does 15 years compare with 30 years at the same rate?

At 4%, the 30-year payment is about $477.42 versus $739.69 on 15 years—a $262.27 monthly gap and about $38,725.68 difference in scheduled interest if both run full term.

What if I add $100.00 extra toward principal each month?

Payoff lands near month 152 with about $27,624.76 in interest—savings versus minimum pay depend on servicer application rules.
