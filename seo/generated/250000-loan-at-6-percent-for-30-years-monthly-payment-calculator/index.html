Scenario lens: shorter horizon trade-offs

Stretching the same $250,000.00 to 15 years at 6% drops the installment to about $2,109.64 but adds roughly $159,859.90 in lifetime interest versus this 30-year note.

At 7.5% instead of 6%, expect about $1,748.04 per month and $379,293.06 total interest.

Is a 30-year loan worth it at 6%?

Worth it when $1,498.88 fits net cash flow after reserves—not when it maxes out DTI. You trade $610.77/mo versus a 15-year schedule for about $159,859.90 less lifetime interest.

Frequently asked questions

What is the monthly payment on $250,000.00 at 6% for 30 years?

About $1,498.88 per month in principal and interest for 360 on-time payments (total repayment about $539,595.47).

How much total interest will I pay?

Roughly $289,595.47 over the full term if you do not prepay.

How does 30 years compare with 15 years at the same rate?

At 6%, the 15-year payment is about $2,109.64 versus $1,498.88 on 30 years—a $610.77 monthly gap and about $159,859.90 difference in scheduled interest if both run full term.

What if I add $200.00 extra toward principal each month?

Payoff lands near month 267 with about $203,362.51 in interest—savings versus minimum pay depend on servicer application rules.
