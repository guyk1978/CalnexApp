    How mortgage underwriting actually works

    By Daniel Morris Updated 2026-05-14 10 min read

    Mortgage strategy hub · Pre-approval vs pre-qualification

    Updated May 2026. Descriptions reflect common conventional workflows; investor overlays vary.

    Verification checklist (what underwriters reconcile)

    1. Income/employment — paystubs, W-2s, tax returns; self-employed files add P&L and transcript patterns.
    2. Assets — sourcing for down payment and reserves; large deposits need paper trails.
    3. Credit — score, depth, derogatories; pricing tiers interact with LLPA-style buckets.
    4. Debts — minimum payments feed DTI; new tradelines before closing can fail QC.
    5. Collateral — appraisal vs contract; title exceptions; property type flags.

    Automated underwriting (AUS) vs human underwriter

    AUS (e.g., Desktop Underwriter / Loan Product Advisor paths) returns approve/eligible style findings with stipulations. A human underwriter validates exceptions, interprets guideline gray zones, and issues conditions you must clear before closing.

    Common “surprise” conditions

    • Updated paystub within 30 days of closing
    • Letter of explanation for credit inquiries
    • Proof a collection account is paid or excluded per program rules

    Where this meets your numbers

    Underwriting does not replace amortization literacy—it decides whether you reach the closing table where that schedule begins.

    Reference sources

    FAQ

    Does pre-approval mean underwriting is finished?

    Usually no—pre-approval is conditional; final sign-off often waits on appraisal, title, and refreshed income/credit windows.

    Why did my “approved” loan get suspended?

    New facts (job change, debt, deposit) can reopen findings—treat approval as stateful until funded.

    Who sets the rules?

    Agency/investor guidelines plus lender overlays—your LO should cite the constraint, not vibes.

    Does appraisal equal value?

    Appraisal is an opinion for the transaction; contract price and investor collateral rules still matter.
