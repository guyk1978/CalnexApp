    Debt-to-income ratio for mortgage qualification: what lenders measure

    By Daniel Morris Updated 2026-05-14 8 min read

    Quick answer

    Quick answer: Front-end DTI = proposed housing payment (P&I + taxes + insurance + HOA + applicable MI) ÷ gross monthly income. Back-end DTI = housing + all recurring debts that hit credit (auto, cards’ minimums, student loans per guideline payment, child support, etc.) ÷ gross income. Automated underwriting may allow higher back-ends when credit, LTV/MI, and reserves are strong—program caps are not a single universal number.

    Worked example (illustrative)

    Line Monthly amount
    Gross income $9,500
    Proposed housing (PITIA) $2,660
    Other obligations counted in DTI $950
    Front-end DTI 2,660 ÷ 9,500 ≈ 28%
    Back-end DTI (2,660 + 950) ÷ 9,500 ≈ 38%

    Utilities, groceries, and childcare not on credit reports usually sit outside DTI—they still matter for your real budget after tax withholdings.

    Student loans, 401(k) loans, co-signed debt

    • Student loans: investors often require a documented payment—even if loans are deferred—not “$0 because I feel fine.”
    • 401(k) loans: payroll repayment often counts as a recurring obligation until paid off.
    • Co-signed debts: may count unless you document 12 months of payments from the other party per program rules.

    Improving DTI before application

    Paying down installment loans with large minimums usually trims back-end DTI faster than micro-paying revolving to low balances (both help credit utilization). Avoid new auto leases or furniture financing in the 90–120 days before underwriting if the payment will report before closing.

    Model housing payment components in the mortgage calculator; understand how interest accrual flows into P&I inside that housing line.

    Pre-approval paperwork

    DTI is only as good as verified income. Self-employed borrowers should align with tax returns and bank statements before shopping—see mortgage pre-approval vs pre-qualification for what is actually verified versus a soft quote.

    Frequently asked questions

    Do lenders use gross or net income for DTI?

    Classic DTI uses gross monthly income; build your household budget on net pay separately.

    What is a “healthy” back-end DTI for conventional loans?

    Many files land in the mid-30s to low-40s back-end for planning, but AUS findings and compensating factors move outcomes—get a loan officer to run real numbers.

    Does paying off a car loan help my mortgage?

    Removing a fixed payment lowers back-end DTI immediately; credit score impact is secondary unless utilization or mix shifts a lot.

    Is my current rent counted in DTI?

    Forward-looking models replace rent with the proposed mortgage payment; overlapping housing (own + rent during transition) can count both until one ends.
