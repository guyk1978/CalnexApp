Mortgage origination fees vs discount points: what each dollar buys

By Daniel Morris Updated 2026-05-14 7 min read

Quick answer

Quick answer: Origination/processing dollars pay the lender’s work; they do not automatically buy a lower rate unless bundled into a priced offer. Discount points are prepaid interest: you pay more at closing to reduce the note rate and monthly interest accrual. Lender credits are the mirror—you accept a higher rate for cash to offset closing costs. Compare everything on months to recover your out-of-pocket, same as refi break-even.

Definitions on the Loan Estimate

  • Section A-type lender charges: underwriting, processing, admin—ask which are negotiable promos vs hard cost.
  • Discount points (% of loan): each point is typically 1% of amount borrowed; the rate reduction per point moves with market pricing (not a fixed 0.25% rule).
  • Credits: show as negative lines; they raise long-run interest expense in exchange for fewer dollars at the closing table.

Worked break-even: one point on $400,000

Illustrative 30-year fixed, balance $400,000—P&I only.

Quote Rate Monthly P&I Upfront discount
Par-ish baseline 6.50% ~$2,528 $0 points
Pay 1.0 discount point 6.25% (illustrative buydown) ~$2,463 $4,000 (1% of $400k)

Monthly P&I drops about $65 in this paired example (~$2,528 → ~$2,463). Simple payback on the $4,000 point check: 4,000 ÷ 65 ≈ 61 months (~5 years) before cumulative payment savings alone recover the buydown—longer once you add lost interest on any financed closing costs. If your actual LE shows a larger payment gap for the same dollar cost, break-even shortens proportionally; if the gap is smaller, break-even extends.

Always pair points with refinance / hold-period break-even—if you sell at year three, points you paid for a 20-year break-even are usually a bad trade.

APR: helpful, incomplete

APR spreads many finance charges into an annualized figure for cross-lender sorting. It still smooths away your hold period, tax treatment, and opportunity cost of cash. Use APR to shortlist, then reconcile Section A+B+C line items before wiring funds.

Negotiation that moves numbers

Parallel Loan Estimates expose duplicate fees and odd title markups. Ask each lender the same question: “What changes if I add or remove 1 point?” Capture screenshots/PDFs with dates—rate locks move markets.

Frequently asked questions

Can I finance discount points?

Often yes by increasing the loan amount, but you then pay interest on the financed points—break-even lengthens.

Are origination fees tax deductible?

Tax treatment for points vs fees changes with law and use-case—ask a CPA; this page is not tax advice.

Do lender credits hurt my APR?

They can raise APR because the rate is higher even though cash-to-close falls—evaluate total cost over your expected months with the loan.

Should a first-time buyer pay points?

Only if the break-even in months fits a realistic keep horizon and reserves stay intact after closing.
